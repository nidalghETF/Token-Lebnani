Token-Lebnani Whitepaper

Democratizing Lebanese Real Estate Investment Through Blockchain Tokenization

Version 1.0 December 2025 Nidal Gharzeddine

1. Executive Summary

Token-Lebnani is a pioneering, regulated platform that bridges Lebanese real estate with the global investment community through blockchain-based tokenization. By enabling property owners to fractionalize their assets into tradeable digital tokens, we unlock liquidity, reduce investment barriers, and create a transparent, compliant marketplace. Our unique value proposition centers on a capital-light, platform-only model operating under the robust ADGM regulatory sandbox, targeting the vast Lebanese diaspora seeking USD-denominated exposure to their homeland's property market.

Unlike traditional REITs or direct property purchases, Token-Lebnani offers sub-$1,000 entry points, 24/7 global trading (post-Year 2), and full digital ownership records. We achieve this without owning assets or handling Lebanese currency (LBP), mitigating key risks associated with the Lebanese market. Our technology partner, DigiShares, provides a proven, compliant infrastructure, allowing us to focus on regulatory compliance, asset sourcing, and investor relations.

2. The Problem

2.1 Illiquidity in Lebanese Real Estate

The Lebanese real estate market faces a severe liquidity crisis. Property owners often struggle to find buyers, especially for high-value assets, leading to long holding periods and difficulty in realizing value. The economic collapse has further exacerbated this, with traditional financing channels largely unavailable.

2.2 Barriers for the Lebanese Diaspora

The Lebanese diaspora represents a significant pool of capital but faces numerous challenges when investing back home:

  • High Entry Costs: Purchasing property often requires substantial capital, excluding smaller investors.
  • Complexity and Trust: Navigating legal title, local processes, and finding reliable partners can be daunting and risky.
  • Lack of Fractional Ownership: No established mechanism exists for owning parts of properties.
  • Currency Risk: Exposure to the volatile Lebanese Pound (LBP).
  • Liquidity Concerns: Exiting a property investment is often a slow and complex process.

3. Our Solution

3.1 Blockchain-Based Tokenization

Token-Lebnani leverages blockchain technology to create digital tokens representing fractional ownership in Lebanese real estate. Each token is backed by shares in a newly formed Lebanese Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) that owns the underlying property. This process is known as tokenization.

3.2 The Token-Lebnani Platform

We act as a regulated intermediary, facilitating the entire process:

  1. Asset Sourcing: Partner with Lebanese property owners seeking liquidity.
  2. SPV Formation: Facilitate the creation of a new, dedicated Lebanese SPV for each property.
  3. Tokenization: Work with legal and technical partners to issue digital tokens representing shares in the SPV.
  4. Investor Onboarding: Conduct KYC/AML checks and onboard global investors (excluding US persons).
  5. Primary Sale: Facilitate the initial sale of tokens to investors.
  6. Secondary Trading (Year 2+): Connect tokens to an ADGM-licensed secondary market for ongoing trading.

3.3 Key Benefits

For Property Owners:

  • Sell portions of their property to raise capital.
  • Access a global pool of investors.
  • Retain control or sell entirely based on their needs.

For Investors:

  • Low minimum investment thresholds (<$1,000).
  • Transparent ownership via blockchain.
  • USD or stablecoin (USDC) pricing.
  • Access to a new asset class.
  • Potential for future liquidity.

4. Technology Stack

4.1 DigiShares Platform

Token-Lebnani utilizes the DigiShares white-label platform, a proven solution for security tokenization. This choice significantly reduces development time and cost while ensuring regulatory compliance.

  • Year 1 (Launch Plan): ERC-1404 tokens on Polygon for low gas fees and compliance.
  • Year 2+ (Standard Plan): Upgrade to ERC-3643 (T-REX) for enhanced features and potentially broader institutional custody support.

4.2 Infrastructure

The platform is hosted on secure, scalable cloud infrastructure managed by DigiShares. We integrate with SumSub for KYC/AML and Stripe/Coinbase for payment processing.

5. Regulatory Framework

5.1 ADGM Regulatory Sandbox

Token-Lebnani will operate under the Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM) Regulatory Laboratory (RegLab) sandbox. This provides a controlled environment to test our innovative model while ensuring compliance with international standards.

  • Activities: Issuing tokens representing ownership in Lebanese SPVs.
  • Limitations (Sandbox): No secondary trading allowed initially, limited number of investors per token, AUM caps.
  • Compliance: Full KYC/AML, investor suitability checks, risk disclosures.

5.2 Lebanese SPV Structure

Each tokenized property is owned by a separate Lebanese SPV. Token holders own shares in this SPV. This structure complies with Lebanese property ownership laws for foreign entities and isolates the platform from direct property ownership risks.

5.3 Exit Strategy (Sandbox to Full License)

After a successful operational period in the sandbox (typically 12-18 months), we plan to apply for a full VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider) license under ADGM, enabling secondary market trading.

6. Go-to-Market Strategy

6.1 Target Audience

Our primary target is the Lebanese diaspora living in compliant jurisdictions (e.g., Canada, Australia, EU, GCC) with an interest in Lebanese real estate. We also target international investors seeking exposure to the Lebanese market.

6.2 Marketing Channels

  • Digital Marketing: Targeted online campaigns focusing on diaspora communities.
  • Community Events: Participation in Lebanese cultural and professional events in target diaspora regions.
  • Content Marketing: Publishing educational content and whitepapers.
  • Partnerships: Collaborating with Lebanese diaspora organizations and financial advisors.

7. Financial Model

7.1 Revenue Streams

  • Issuance Fee: A percentage (e.g., 3%) of the total tokenized value, paid by the property owner.
  • Primary Market Fees: Buyer and seller fees applied during the initial token sale (e.g., 2.5% each).
  • Secondary Market Fees (Year 2+): Buyer and seller fees applied on trades in the secondary market (e.g., 1.5% each).

8. Risks & Mitigation

8.1 Regulatory Risk

Risk: Changes in ADGM or Lebanese regulations, or failure to exit the sandbox.

Mitigation: Maintain close engagement with regulators, use experienced legal counsel, and ensure full compliance from day one.

8.2 Title Risk

Risk: Disputed ownership or legal encumbrances on the underlying property.

Mitigation: Mandatory pre-tokenization legal opinion for every property, clear disclaimers, and owner indemnity clauses.

8.3 Market Risk

Risk: Low demand for tokens or lack of secondary market liquidity.

Mitigation: Careful asset selection, targeted marketing to diaspora, and establishing a connection to a reputable secondary market venue.

8.4 Technology Risk

Risk: Platform downtime, security breaches, or blockchain issues.

Mitigation: Use reputable technology partners (DigiShares, cloud providers), implement robust security measures, and have contingency plans.

8.5 Reputational Risk

Risk: Confusion about the platform's role vs. asset ownership.

Mitigation: Clear, consistent disclaimers across all materials: "Token-Lebnani is a platform facilitator. Tokens represent shares in Lebanese SPVs, not ownership of Token-Lebnani or direct property ownership."

By combining blockchain technology with a robust regulatory framework and a deep understanding of the Lebanese diaspora market, we aim to create a transparent, efficient, and accessible platform. Our success depends on meticulous execution, strong regulatory relationships, and the ability to source high-quality assets. We are committed to building a trusted and compliant bridge between Lebanese real estate and global capital.
