Legal & Technical Framework

Compliance Architecture for Cross-Border Real Estate Tokenization

1. DUAL JURISDICTION STRUCTURE

Our legal framework is built on a dual-jurisdiction model that separates operational risk from asset ownership, ensuring maximum protection for investors and regulatory compliance.

2. THE REGULATORY PATHWAY

Our Strategy: From Sandbox to Full License.

The legal structure of Token Lebnani is not arbitrary; it is architected specifically to navigate the FSRA RegLab Sandbox while adhering to strict capital requirements.

Phase 1: The Sandbox Entry (Year 1-2)

  • Status: We are applying to enter the FSRA RegLab Sandbox as a Category 4 (Arranging Deals) entity.
  • The Capital Requirement: We will meet the $10,000 USD Base Capital requirement for Cat 4.
  • The Trade-Off: To maintain this efficient category and avoid the higher capital costs of a Multilateral Trading Facility (MTF), we voluntarily enforce a 2-Year Lock-Up on all tokens.

Phase 2: The Rollout (Year 2-3)

  • Status: Upon successful Sandbox validation, we will transition to a Full Category 4 License.
  • Liquidity Strategy: As the platform matures, we will assess the need for secondary market liquidity.
  • The Solution: We intend to outsource secondary trading to an existing ADGM-licensed MTF rather than becoming an exchange ourselves. This allows us to provide liquidity without taking on the regulatory burden of an Exchange.

Phase 3: Full Operations (Year 3+)

  • Status: Full Category 4 (Arranging) License.
  • Expansion: Horizontal to Egypt (Mega Market) and Vertical to SME Equity.

3. THE TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE (DIGISHARES & SMART CONTRACTS)

The Technology Layer: Digishares White-Label

We utilize the Digishares Standard White-Label solution to ensure institutional-grade security and compliance. This separates the Platform Operator (Token Lebnani) from the Technology Risk.

  • Issuance Module: Handles the creation of the Smart Contracts and the minting of ERC-20 tokens (or equivalent standard).
  • Wallet Integration: Non-custodial connection (WalletConnect) allowing investors to link MetaMask, Trust Wallet, or Ledger hardware wallets directly to the platform. Token Lebnani never touches user private keys.
  • STO Management: Automates the reporting and compliance checks required for the Security Token Offering.
  • Dormant Secondary Market: The Digishares platform includes secondary trading modules, but we will hard-disable this feature in Phase 1 via the Smart Contract (see Lock-Up below).

The Smart Contract Layer: Programmed Equity

The token itself is not just a database entry; it is a Smart Contract on a public blockchain.

  • Lock-Up Mechanism: A transferEnabled boolean is hard-coded into the contract. For the first 2 years, this function returns false.
  • The Effect: Even if an investor transfers their tokens to another wallet, the receiving wallet cannot sell or move them. The tokens are "frozen" in the ecosystem until the DAO or Keyholder unlocks them.
  • Dividend Distribution: The contract includes a distribute function. This allows the SPV's FMC to pay rent revenues in USDT/USDC directly to the token holders' wallets automatically, without Token Lebnani's involvement.

4. THE CORPORATE STRUCTURE (THE ASSET LAYER)

To ensure perfect risk isolation and legal clarity, we utilize a Multi-Layer Corporate Hierarchy.

Layer 1: The ADGM Holding Company

  • Entity: Token Lebnani (LTD).
  • Role: The Platform Operator and License Holder.
  • Domicile: ADGM, Al Maryah Island.
  • Function: Holds the IP, runs the Digishares platform interface, manages compliance, and holds the Cat 4 License.
  • Risk: It holds zero assets. If the platform fails, the assets are safe.

Layer 2: The Asset SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle)

  • Entity: "Property Alpha SPV LLC" (Located in Lebanon).
  • Role: The legal owner of the physical property.
  • Structure: The SPV is established specifically to hold title to one building (e.g., a 10-unit apartment block in Beirut).
  • Ownership: Token Lebnani (Holding Company) does not own the SPV. The Investors (Token Holders) own the SPV collectively.
  • Why it matters: If Building A has structural issues or lawsuits, the liability dies with that SPV. It cannot bleed into the Holding Company or the SPV for Building B.

Layer 3: The Facilities Management Company (FMC)

  • Entity: Independent Third-Party Contractor.
  • Role: Property Management.
  • Function: They collect rent, fix elevators, manage tenants, and pay utility bills.
  • The Cash Flow: Rent is paid into the SPV bank account. The FMC takes their fee, and the Net Operating Income (NOI) is distributed to token holders.
  • Separation: Token Lebnani (You) does not manage toilets or chase tenants. We outsource this operational headache to professionals to ensure the asset retains value.

5. THE LEGAL CONTRACT LAYER

The Smart Contract defines the tech rights, but the Token Purchase Agreement (TPA) defines the legal rights. This is the "Other Contract" you must have.

The Token Purchase Agreement (TPA)

Before an investor can buy a token, they must sign/click-accept the TPA. This is a digital contract governed by ADGM Law.

Key Clauses:

  • Representation: The investor confirms they are a "Professional Investor" or "High Net Worth Individual" and understand the illiquid nature of the asset.
  • Enforceability: The investor acknowledges that the Token represents a contractual right to the profits of the Lebanese SPV, as per Lebanese Law.
  • Waiver of Liability: The investor waives liability against Token Lebnani for property-specific issues (e.g., a tenant defaults, a pipe bursts). Their recourse is solely against the SPV assets.
  • Smart Contract Reference: The TPA explicitly states that the Lock-Up and Dividend functions in the Smart Contract are the mechanical execution of these legal rights.
