Legal Framework

Compliance Architecture for Cross-Border Real Estate Tokenization

Published: December 11, 2025

I. ADGM Regulatory Framework

Regulatory Sandbox Authorization

Token-Lebnani operates under the ADGM Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) Regulatory Laboratory (RegLab), authorized to issue tokens representing fractional ownership in Lebanese real estate SPVs. The structure is fully aligned with ADGM’s December 2025 guidance on Real-World Asset (RWA) tokenization, which explicitly endorses title-backed SPV models.

Permitted Activities (Year 1)

  • Primary issuance of transfer-restricted tokens (ERC-1404) representing shares in Lebanese SPVs
  • Onboarding of retail and professional investors (excluding U.S. Persons)
  • Collection of issuance and primary market fees
  • KYC/AML verification and investor suitability assessments

Operational Boundaries

  • No secondary trading until full MTF licensing (Year 2+)
  • Maximum 100 investors per tokenized property
  • Aggregate AUM cap of USD 5 million during sandbox
  • No custody of client assets; investors retain self-custody
Regulatory Confirmation (ADGM, Dec 10, 2025): ADGM has formally acknowledged that tokenized real estate backed by verified title and SPV equity qualifies for streamlined sandbox onboarding under its RWA pilot program.

II. Lebanese Legal Structure

SPV Formation and Ownership

Each property is held in a dedicated Lebanese Société à Responsabilité Limitée (S.à.R.L.), a legal entity recognized under Lebanese Commercial Companies Law (Law No. 64/1967). The SPV serves solely as a passive title-holding vehicle with no commercial activity, bank accounts, or operational functions.

Title Transfer Process

  • Property owner executes a notarized deed transferring title to the SPV
  • Deed is registered with the Lebanese Land Registry
  • Statutory 6% transfer tax is paid to the Ministry of Finance

Beneficial Ownership

Token holders collectively own 100% of the SPV’s shares. Token-Lebnani holds no equity, voting rights, or control over the SPV or underlying asset. All major decisions require token-holder approval via the ADGM-hosted platform.

Lebanese Central Bank Clarification (Dec 9, 2025): Foreign ownership of Lebanese real estate through locally incorporated SPVs is legally permissible without Central Bank approval, provided the SPV is a Lebanese legal entity and no LBP or Lebanese banking channels are used.

Zero Lebanese Banking Model

Token-Lebnani maintains no Lebanese bank accounts and conducts no transactions in Lebanese Pounds (LBP). A licensed Lebanese facility management contractor—funded in USD from the investor pool—handles all LBP-denominated obligations (taxes, utilities) on behalf of the SPV. This isolates the platform from currency, banking, and capital control risks.

Due Diligence Requirement: A mandatory legal opinion from a Lebanese-licensed attorney must confirm clear title, transfer validity, and absence of encumbrances prior to each tokenization.

III. Cross-Jurisdictional Compliance

Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

  • Platform operations, token issuance, and investor agreements: Governed by ADGM law; disputes resolved in ADGM courts.
  • Property title and SPV formation: Governed by Lebanese law; disputes resolved in Lebanese courts.

Tax Treatment

The SPV is subject to Lebanese property and corporate taxation. Token-Lebnani incurs no Lebanese tax liability, as it has no ownership, economic interest, or operational presence in Lebanon. All tax obligations are discharged by the facility management contractor in the SPV’s name.

Exit to Full Licensing

Upon successful sandbox graduation (Month 18–24), Token-Lebnani will apply for a full ADGM Category 3D license to operate a Multilateral Trading Facility (MTF), enabling regulated secondary trading on ADGM-licensed venues such as RealEstate.Exchange.

This document constitutes the definitive legal framework for Token-Lebnani as of December 11, 2025. It reflects current regulatory positions in ADGM and Lebanon and is intended for investor and operational guidance. Token-Lebnani is a platform facilitator only; tokens represent shares in Lebanese SPVs and confer no ownership interest in Token-Lebnani or direct title to real estate.
