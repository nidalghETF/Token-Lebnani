Token-Lebnani Value Proposition

Regulated · Retail-First · Lebanon-Focused

Published: December 11, 2025

1. The Problem: A Market Trapped in Crisis

Lebanon’s real estate sector is immobilized:

  • Illiquidity: High-value properties sit unsold for years—no single buyers, no mortgage market, and collapsing demand.
  • Barriers for Savers: Locals and diaspora want to preserve wealth in real estate but face $100K+ entry points, opaque titles, and zero fractional options.
  • Capital Controls: Transferring funds to/from Lebanon is heavily restricted. The Lebanese Pound (LBP) is volatile and unreliable for investment.
  • Bureaucratic Delays: Property registration can take months, cost 6% in transfer taxes, and lacks digital infrastructure.

Yet opportunity exists: real estate prices are at historic lows, and a peace-driven recovery could unlock significant upside. The market needs liquidity without systemic risk, and access without LBP exposure.

2. Our Solution: Fractional, Regulated, USD-Denominated

Token-Lebnani is the first ADGM-regulated platform enabling fractional ownership of Lebanese real estate—for global retail investors, in USD or USDC, and without touching Lebanese banks.

We do not own, manage, or finance any property. Instead, we facilitate:

  1. SPV Creation: A new Lebanese S.à.R.L. (SPV) is formed per property—legally permitted under Lebanese law for foreign ownership.
  2. Title Transfer: Owner deeds the property to the SPV via notarized sale and land registry update.
  3. Token Issuance: Shares in the SPV are tokenized on a compliant blockchain (ERC-1404 → ERC-3643).
  4. Global Access: Investors from the diaspora (Canada, EU, GCC, Australia) buy tokens from under $1,000.
  5. Zero LBP Model: All USD funds flow through non-Lebanese accounts. A local contractor handles LBP obligations (taxes, utilities) on behalf of the SPV—Token-Lebnani never handles LBP.

3. Why Now? Regulatory Tailwinds

  • ADGM’s RWA Push (Dec 2025): ADGM has accelerated its sandbox pathway for real estate tokenization, with explicit support for title-backed SPV structures—exactly our model.
  • Lebanon’s Clarification (Dec 9, 2025): The Central Bank confirmed that foreign ownership via Lebanese SPVs is legal and unrestricted (outside security zones)—removing prior ambiguity.

Together, these developments validate Token-Lebnani’s cross-border architecture as both compliant and timely.
